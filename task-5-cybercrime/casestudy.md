# Cybercrime Case Study – UPI Payment Fraud

Nowadays, UPI fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes in India. In this type of scam, criminals trick people into transferring money through fake payment requests or phishing messages. 

The fraud begins when a scammer contacts the person through a phone call, SMS, or social media message. The scammer may pretend to be a bank official, customer support agent, or online seller. They often send a payment request link or ask the victim to scan a QR code.

Many people believe scanning the code will help them receive money, but it actually sends money from their bank account. Students and elderly people are often targeted because they may not fully understand how digital payments work.

Once the money is transferred, it is quickly moved between multiple accounts, making recovery difficult. Victims may suffer financial loss and emotional stress.

This case shows the importance of digital literacy and awareness about safe online financial practices.
